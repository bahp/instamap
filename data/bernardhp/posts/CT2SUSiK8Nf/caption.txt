🔘The Longmen Grottoes🔘
⚫located south of present-day Luoyang in Henan province, China.
⚫In total, there is more than 2,300 grottoes and niches, 100,000 Buddhist statues, ranging in size from several centimetres to over 17 meters, and over 2,800 inscriptions carved on steles in the site, making it the largest such complex in China.
⚫They are some of the finest examples of Chinese Buddhist art.
⚫The grottoes or caves were excavated and carved with Buddhist subjects over the period from 493 AD to 1127 AD.
⚫The earliest history of the creation of Longmen Grottoes is traced to the reign of Emperor Xiaowen of Northern Wei dynasty.
