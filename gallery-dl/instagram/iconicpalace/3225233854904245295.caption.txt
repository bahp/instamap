1. History: Stahleck Castle was constructed in the 12th century, around 1135, by the Archbishop of Cologne. It served as a toll castle, collecting fees from passing ships on the Rhine River.

2. Architecture: The castle is known for its well-preserved Romanesque architecture, including thick stone walls and a central keep. It has a distinctive appearance with multiple towers and a courtyard.

3. Location: The castle is perched on a hill overlooking the Rhine River, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. It is a popular stop along the Rhine River's scenic route.

4. Functions: Over the centuries, Stahleck Castle has served various purposes, including as a royal residence, a military fortress, and even a youth hostel in recent times.
